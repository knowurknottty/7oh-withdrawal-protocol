## What Is 7-OH?

7-Hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) is a synthetic, concentrated opioid-like alkaloid derived from kratom. It is:
- 30-40x more potent than natural kratom leaf
- Acts on the same mu-opioid receptors as prescription opioids
- Sold at gas stations, smoke shops, and convenience stores
- Marketed as "natural" or "herbal" — it is NOT comparable to kratom tea
- The DEA has filed notice to schedule 7-OH; earliest ban date: August 5, 2026

**Key insight:** 7-OH withdrawal is more intense than natural kratom withdrawal and can be comparable to or worse than prescription opioid withdrawal.

### Phase 2: Active Taper Protocol

**Goal:** Reduce 7-OH dose gradually while maintaining supplement stack.

#### Taper Schedule

**General Principles:**
- Reduce dose by 10-15% every 7-10 days
- If symptoms spike, HOLD at current dose for 5-7 days before continuing
- Slower is better — there is no prize for speed
- Track everything in a journal or app

**For 7-OH Tablets:**
1. Weigh tablets on a milligram scale
2. Shave tablets with nail file or razor
3. Reduce by 1-2mg per 7-10 day cycle
4. Example: 5 tabs/day × 20mg = 100mg → reduce to 98mg → 96mg → etc.

**For Kratom Powder (if transitioning):**
1. Weigh each dose on a digital scale
2. Reduce by 0.5g every 3-5 days
3. Example: 10g → 9.5g → 9g → etc.

**For Tonic Drinks:**
1. Measure precisely (59mL per bottle)
2. Reduce by 2mL every 3 days
3. Save removed portions for future doses

## Emergency Protocol (Severe Symptoms)

If experiencing:
- Inability to keep fluids down for 24+ hours
- Seizures
- Suicidal ideation
- Severe dehydration
- Chest pain or irregular heartbeat

**Actions:**
1. Go to emergency room
2. Tell them: "I am withdrawing from 7-hydroxymitragynine, an opioid-like substance"
3. Ask for: IV fluids, anti-nausea medication (ondansetron), clonidine if available
4. Do NOT attempt to taper further until stabilized
